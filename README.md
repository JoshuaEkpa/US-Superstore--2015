# US-Superstore--2015
This study presents a data-driven evaluation of retail sales performance for the 2015 financial year using quantitative analytical methods implemented in Microsoft Excel. The analysis investigates total revenue, product category performance, regional sales distribution, temporal sales trends, and profitability to support evidence-based decision-making. Secondary sales data were examined using descriptive statistics, pivot table analysis, trend value estimation, and confidence interval analysis.

The results indicate that the store generated approximately $19.24 million in total revenue during the study period. The Technology category accounted for the highest share of unit sales (39.68%), followed by Furniture (34.64%) and Office Supplies (25.68%). Regional analysis shows that the North Central United States contributed the largest proportion of revenue (30.77%), while the Southern region recorded the lowest contribution (18.56%)
